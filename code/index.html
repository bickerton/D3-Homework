Correlations Discovered Between Health Care and Poverty

In general, there appears to be a linear correlation between the variables: as poverty increases, the percentage of people without without health care also increases.

The percentage of people in poverty has a range between 9% and 22%, while the range of people without health care ranges between 2% and 25%. Both variables have a roughly even distribution throughout the data.

There are exceptions apparent in the trends. One example being Texas, where those without health care are around 25% but the poverty level is less than 20%. There may be other factors to consider for this discrepancy.
